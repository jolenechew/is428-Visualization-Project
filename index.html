Enhancing Disaster Preparedness:
Earthquake & Tsunami Visualization


Analyzing Earthquakes in real-time, and its relation to tsunamis, in an effort to educate and raise awareness on the severity of earthquakes.

There are on average 20,000 earthquakes per year, causing nearly 2.3 million deaths since 1990, where 28 percent of these deaths were due to secondary effects like tsunamis. Earthquakes and tsunamis can cause huge catastrophes, even as countries now are equipped with higher disaster preparedness. To minimize the extent of damage, we need to first know more about these disasters. Through innovative and deep analysis and visualization, our project aims to enhance the disaster preparedness of countries and inspire action across countries to mitigate the devastating effects of earthquakes.

Let's first understand what are earthquakes and tsunamis.

How Earthquakes form:

The earth consists of tectonic plates. These plates are always moving. The edges of these plates are rough and the tectonic plates could get stuck, while the rest of the plate continues to move. During this period the energy that is normally used to keep the plates moving is stacked up. If the rest of the plate has moved far enough and the plates have overcome their friction, then the plates are not stuck anymore and all the stored energy goes as seismic waves to all directions. These waves will shake the ground and an earthquake will occur.

How Tsunamis form:

A tsunami is a series of waves that are crossing the oceans with a high speed. This speed gets larger when the water depth increases. If the water depth is 4.5 km then the tsunami speed can go up to 900 km/h. If the water depth decreases then the velocity of the tsunami decreases. If these waves hit a coast, then a runup will occur.
A run-up is the vertical increase in the height of the water level. This increase could go up to 30 meters and higher in extreme cases. The most frequent cause are the earthquakes. The seismic waves that went through the earth are then given to the ocean, and the waves arise. In this study, we look at tsunamis caused by earthquakes and their relation.
