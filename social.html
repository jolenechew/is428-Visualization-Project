Social Factors

An analysis of how social factors like population density, median age etc. affect the extent of damages a country suffers.




The World Health Organization has classified older adults as a demographic particularly susceptible to the impacts of natural disasters. They tend to experience higher mortality rates in the wake of earthquakes and tsunamis compared to other age groups due to potential mobility limitations and preexisting medical conditions.


Countries with an older population and a higher population density tend to suffer more extensive damage from these natural disasters.
